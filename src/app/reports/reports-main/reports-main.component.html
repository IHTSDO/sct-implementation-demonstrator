SNOMED CT Changes reports

Maintaining the integrity and usefulness of a clinical terminology like SNOMED CT requires continuous updates, including concept inactivations, fully specified name (FSN) changes, and the introduction of new concepts. These updates ensure that the terminology remains aligned with clinical practice, evolving medical knowledge, and interoperability needs across health systems. This page provides access to detailed reports of these changes, offering transparency for implementers, terminology managers, and system developers who rely on SNOMED CT for clinical decision support, coding, and analytics.

This aligns with the principle of graceful evolution, which emphasizes that terminologies should change in a way that minimizes disruption to users. SNOMED CT follows this principle by ensuring that inactivated concepts have clear historical relationships to their replacements, FSN changes history is fully tracked, and new concepts integrate seamlessly into existing hierarchies. To further support users in managing terminology changes, SNOMED CT provides several tools and features that facilitate adaptation. Historical associations link inactive concepts to their active equivalents, ensuring continuity in clinical records and analytics. The Expression Constraint Language (ECL) includes history functions that leverages the historical associations in queries, making it easier to include inactive content in clinical queries. Additionally, SNOMED International offers structured reports and tooling that help implementers integrate updates seamlessly into their systems. These mechanisms ensure that SNOMED CT remains a reliable and evolving standard while minimizing the impact of inevitable terminology changes.
